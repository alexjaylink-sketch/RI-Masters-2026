📋 Tournament Overview

Welcome to the RI Masters — a one-day, 18-hole team triathlon. Two-person teams will navigate three distinct formats across the round, testing every facet of partnership golf. Whether you sign up as a team or request a random partner, every player will swing their own club and contribute to the outcome.

🖊️ Sign-Up: Players may register as a pre-formed two-person team or sign up individually to be paired randomly with another participant. Random pairs will be drawn and announced before the round.

🏌️ The 18-Hole Team Triathlon

One cumulative net score across all 18 holes. Lowest total wins. The format changes every six holes — here's how each leg works:

Leg 1
Shamble
Holes 1 – 6
"Best drive, then you're on your own."
Leg 2
Modified Chapman
Holes 7 – 12
"Hit mine, I'll hit yours."
Leg 3
Four-Ball
Holes 13 – 18
"No safety nets. Own it."

LEG 1 Shamble — Holes 1–6

  1. Both players tee off.
  2. The team selects the best drive.
  3. Both players drop and play their own ball from that spot (within one club length, same lie condition — rough stays rough, fairway stays fairway).
  4. Each player holes out with their own ball.
  5. The lower net score between the two is the team score for the hole.

LEG 2 Modified Chapman — Holes 7–12

  1. Both players tee off.
  2. Swap balls: Player A hits Player B's tee shot. Player B hits Player A's tee shot.
  3. After both second shots, select the best-positioned ball.
  4. From there, alternate shots into the hole. The player whose second shot was not chosen hits the third shot, then alternate through to the hole.
  5. Record the team's net score.

LEG 3 Four-Ball — Holes 13–18

  1. Both players play their own ball for the entire hole — no swaps, no shared drives.
  2. The lower net score between the two is the team score for the hole.
  3. If both make the same net score, take that score.

📊 Handicap System

Each player finds their individual course handicap using the chart below. Then the team's handicap is simply the average of the two (rounded to the nearest whole number). That one team handicap applies to all 18 holes, across every leg — no exceptions, no math gymnastics.

Rule Applies To Detail
Team Handicap All 18 holes, every leg Average of both players' course handicaps from the chart, rounded normally. That single number is the team's handicap for the round.
Stroke Allocation All 18 holes Strokes fall on the hardest handicap holes per the scorecard. A team handicap of 10 receives one stroke on handicap holes 1–10.
Net Score Every hole Team gross score minus any handicap stroke(s) received on that hole = team net score for that hole.
🧮 Example: Player A has a 12 course handicap, Player B has an 8. The team handicap is 10 (average of 12 + 8). The team gets 10 strokes across the round — one stroke on each of the 10 hardest handicap holes on the scorecard. Subtract one stroke there from the team's gross score. Same rule for every leg. No segment math required.

📜 Course Rules

Improved Lies & Impeded Swings

Improve your lie as needed to give yourself the best opportunity to hit a great golf shot. This includes moving the ball out of a divot or away from obstacles (rocks, roots, etc.) to avoid damaging your club. If the ball is in the rough, leave it in the rough — you may not move it from the rough onto the fairway.

Maximum Score

Triple bogey (par + 3) is the maximum score on any hole. Once you've reached that score, pick up and move on.

Gimmies

Any putt less than 3 feet in length is considered good with the next stroke.

Out of Bounds or Water Ball

If your ball goes out of bounds or into a water hazard, take a lateral drop at the point of entry and add a one-stroke penalty.

Lost Ball

If a ball is clearly agreed by others in your group to be in play but cannot be found (thick grass, another group picked it up, etc.), take a free drop with no penalty and play on. Keep the pace of play moving.

Mulligans

Each player receives one mulligan on the front nine and one on the back nine. Mulligans may be used on any shot (not just tee shots). Declare the mulligan before your next swing.
